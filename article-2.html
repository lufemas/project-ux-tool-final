What is a Binary Search Tree?

Data structure is an important topic to learn in order to enhance your coding accuracy and ability to analyze your code. We will continue our discussion of data structure with another popular data structure called the binary search tree.

Binary Search Tree is a tree-like data structure that branches from a parent node to its child nodes. There are few properties that makes binary search tree a little bit different from other types of trees:

  1. Each node (parent node) can only have up to 2 child nodes
  2. The two child nodes are often referred to as left child and right child where the value of the left child is always less than the parent and the right child is always greater than equal to the parent
  3. Each child must also be a binary search tree

Types of Binary Search Tree

Complete BST

  • All levels (except possibly the last level) maximum number of child nodes
  • All nodes at the last level appears as far left as possible

Full BST

  • Every non-leaf nodes (nodes at the ends) are filled with left child and right child

Types of Traversal

Pre-Order

  • Get the root
  • Traverse the left
  • Traverse the right

Post-Order

  • Traverse the left
  • Traverse the right
  • Get the root
